Acute Fever & Cough — Patient Handout

This handout provides immediate advice for people experiencing fever and cough. It does not replace a medical consultation.

Summary

You have fever and cough. These symptoms most commonly reflect a viral respiratory illness (e.g., common cold, influenza, COVID‑19), but can also be due to bronchitis, asthma exacerbation, or other causes. Monitor your symptoms closely.

Immediate Self‑Care
  • Rest: Reduce activity and get extra rest.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of fluids—water, broths, or oral rehydration solutions.
  • Fever control: Use paracetamol (acetaminophen) or ibuprofen per product instructions for comfort.
  • Cough relief: Warm fluids, throat lozenges, humidified air, and honey (for children >1 year) may help.
When To Seek Urgent Care
  • Shortness of breath, difficulty breathing, or rapid breathing.
  • High fever unresponsive to medication or fever lasting more than 48–72 hours.
  • Chest pain, coughing up blood, confusion, or inability to stay awake.
  • Infants, elderly, or immunocompromised patients with new or worsening symptoms—seek evaluation promptly.
Recommended Next Steps
  • Consider COVID‑19 and influenza testing according to local guidance.
  • If you have asthma, use your inhaler as prescribed and seek care if symptoms do not improve with reliever medication.
  • Arrange follow‑up with primary care (telehealth or in‑person) within 48–72 hours if symptoms persist or worsen.
Contact & Booking

If you would like help booking an appointment with a nearby clinician, use the "Book Appointment" link on your dashboard or contact your clinic directly.

This information is for general guidance only. For personalized medical advice, contact your healthcare provider.
